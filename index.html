Mining & Resources April 20, 2026
Agnico Eagle Mines Ltd acquires Rupert Resources Ltd for US$2.9B
All Stock, Dilutive  ·  Finland — Central Lapland Greenstone Belt
C$2.9B
All Stock · Dilutive
Deal Structure: All Stock, Dilutive
Parties and Advisors:
Target: Rupert Resources (RUP)
Advisors: BMO Capital Markets
(Fairness opinion to Rupert board)
Special Committee Valuator: Origin Merchant Partners
Buyer: Agnico Eagle (AEM)
Advisors:
  • TD Securities Inc.
  • Edgehill Advisory Ltd.
Valuation Metrics:
  • 67% premium compared to RUP's closing share price prior to the announcement, as of April 17, 2026, the last trading day prior to announcement
  • 0.0401 of AEM common share per RUP share. Each share was valued at CAD 12.083 (USD 8.824)
  • RUP shareholders receive up to C$3.00 in additional cash, paid in three C$1.00 tranches tied to hitting 5M oz in reserves, then 7.5M oz combined reserves and production, then 10M oz
Rationale:

AEM bought the rest of stake in Ruper Resources to strengthen its operations regarding gold production. The transaction has as objective consolidating assets in Finland's Central Lapland Greenstone Belt.

This transaction complemented the acquisition of Aurion Resources, B2Gold's 70% stake at Feingold. Combining the deals, AEM now controls over a 2,492 square-kilometer land package around the Ikkari gold discovery, one of Europe's largest undeveloped deposits, improving development coordination and increasing long-term production visibility in a Tier-1 jurisdiction.

Jefferies said the move underscores AEM's strategy of building regional platforms to unlock operational synergies, rather than pursuing geographically dispersed acquisitions.

The firm's rationale is to eliminate fragmented ownership, accelerate development, and extract operational synergies of up to USD 365 million.

Overall, the deal continues the desire of AEM to control high quality jurisdictions and use Finland as its main extraction location in Europe.

Analyst View
"The deal makes strategic sense as AEM already owned 13.9% of RUP. However, the Ikkari region is not yet an operating mine. Therefore, Agnico structured it so RUP shareholders only get the full C$15.00 if the project is delivered. That protects Agnico, making the deal buyer-friendly."
Jurisdiction: Ikkari Gold Discovery — Finland, Central Lapland Greenstone Belt (Tier-1)
