Real Estate / Private Equity April 16, 2026
KingSett Capital & Choice Properties REIT acquire First Capital REIT for C$9.4B
Cash-and-Stock  ·  Canada — Retail Real Estate
C$9.4B
Cash & Stock · Take-Private + Portfolio Split
Deal Structure: Cash-and-Stock. KingSett acquires all First Capital units (taking the REIT private); Choice Properties simultaneously acquires a carved-out portfolio of ~$5.0B in necessity-based retail assets from First Capital.
Parties and Advisors:
Target: First Capital REIT (TSX: FCR.UN)
Advisors: RBC Capital Markets, National Bank Capital Markets
(Fairness opinions to Special Committee and Board)
Special Committee: Independent trustees — unanimously recommended the Transaction
Buyers: KingSett Capital & Choice Properties REIT (TSX: CHP.UN)
KingSett Advisors: TD Securities Inc., Desjardins
(Fully underwritten debt financing)
KingSett Vehicle: KingSett Real Estate Growth LP No. 8
Valuation Metrics:
  • Total consideration of $24.40 per First Capital unit — comprising $19.24 in cash and 0.3186 Choice Properties units per FCR unit
  • 17% premium to First Capital's 20-day volume-weighted average price through April 15, 2026
  • 8% premium to First Capital's Net Asset Value of $22.57 per unit
  • 21% premium to First Capital's 90-day volume-weighted average price
  • Choice Properties acquires ~$5.0B of necessity-based neighbourhood shopping centres; KingSett acquires ~$4.4B of mixed assets including high-street retail, development sites, and enclosed malls
Background:

First Capital REIT is a Toronto-based REIT founded in 1994, owning 136 grocery-anchored, open-air retail properties totalling 21.8M sq ft and $9.3B in total assets across urban Canadian neighbourhoods. The REIT reported 97.1% occupancy and 5.2% same-property NOI growth in FY2025, reflecting a portfolio that had undergone a significant operational turnaround following several years of trading at a NAV discount in the public markets. The deal came together after KingSett approached First Capital unexpectedly, with First Capital's CEO noting management had not been actively pursuing a sale.

Choice Properties REIT (TSX: CHP.UN) is Canada's largest REIT by gross leasable area, owning 756 properties totalling 68M sq ft, primarily leased to Loblaw Companies Limited, with 63% owned by George Weston Limited, Loblaw's parent company. KingSett Capital is a Toronto-based private real estate firm founded in 2002, managing $4.9B in assets across acquisition, development, and financing of Canadian commercial real estate, with flagship properties including Scotia Plaza in downtown Toronto.

Buyers' Rationale:

Choice Properties

Choice Properties sought to acquire the more mature, stable compounding assets from First Capital's portfolio — specifically, its necessity-based retail real estate: properties anchored by grocery stores, drugstores, or banks that make them essential, recurring destinations for consumers. These centres are located in urban neighbourhoods with the strongest demographics in Canada, materially strengthening Choice Properties' national retail portfolio with exposure of approximately 83% and 92% to major Canadian metro markets, while also increasing third-party retail tenant exposure by nearly 50%. Altogether, this represents higher-quality, more defensive cashflow, broadens their third-party tenant relationships (creating potential to deploy those retailers across their existing portfolio), and further consolidates their position as Canada's leading REIT.

KingSett Capital

KingSett Capital acquired the more opportunistic side of First Capital's portfolio — specifically, enclosed shopping malls, development sites, and high-street retail assets, including First Capital's properties in Toronto's Yorkville neighbourhood. This is consistent with KingSett's model as a private equity real estate firm that targets value-add and development opportunities rather than purely stabilized income assets. Notably, KingSett is the formal acquirer of First Capital's units — meaning they are taking the REIT private entirely — which gives them the operational flexibility to reposition and redevelop assets without the scrutiny and short-term pressures of public market ownership. The acquisition is being funded through KingSett Real Estate Growth LP No. 8, a vehicle oriented toward higher-risk, event-driven opportunities, distinguishing it from KingSett's other funds which focus on stable core or residential assets.

Seller's Rationale:

First Capital's decision to accept the transaction was straightforward: the offer represented a 17% premium to its 20-day volume-weighted average price and an 8% premium to Net Asset Value — a compelling immediate return for unitholders that the Special Committee, advised by RBC Capital Markets and National Bank Capital Markets, unanimously endorsed. Unitholders also retain continued participation in the upside through their rollover into Choice Properties units, rather than receiving a purely cash exit.

What makes the rationale more notable is that this was not a sale First Capital had been actively pursuing — KingSett approached the REIT unexpectedly, and management engaged. The board's decision to recommend the transaction reflects their view that the offer fairly captured the value of a portfolio that had undergone a significant operational turnaround, and that delivering that value immediately to unitholders was in their best interest.

AV
Analyst Insight
“This transaction was fair and well-structured for all three parties involved. Both KingSett and Choice Properties received precisely what their respective strategies called for: high-quality assets in strong locations, cleanly allocated between a value-add/development mandate and a core compounding one. From an acquisition standpoint, the timing was opportunistic — the broader Canadian REIT sector has been trading at a significant discount to NAV since interest rates began rising aggressively in 2022, creating a window for well-capitalised private buyers to acquire public real estate assets at below-intrinsic-value prices. The 14% sector-wide NAV discount reported by RBC Capital Markets in mid-2025 illustrates just how wide that gap had become relative to the long-term average of roughly 2%. For First Capital, the deal was equally rational. Rather than continuing to trade at a discount and waiting an uncertain amount of time for public markets to re-rate the sector, unitholders received an immediate and tangible premium to both market price and NAV. The mixed consideration — cash plus Choice Properties units — further allowed unitholders to monetise today while retaining optionality on the continued upside of the combined portfolio. In that sense, the transaction did not force a binary choice between liquidity and long-term participation.”
Jurisdiction: Canada — British Columbia, Alberta, Ontario, Quebec (136 urban neighbourhoods across 4 provinces)

Healthcare / Private Equity April 1, 2026
OMERS Private Equity sale of CBI Health's home care business to Extendicare
All-Cash, Private Equity Partial Divestiture  ·  Canada — Home Health Services
C$570M
All Cash · Partial Exit
Deal Structure: All-Cash, Acquisition, Private Equity Partial Divestiture
Parties and Advisors:
Exit Firm: OMERS Private Equity Inc.
Target: CBI Home Health
Advisors: CIBC Capital Markets
Buyer: Extendicare Inc.
Advisors: TD Securities Inc.
Valuation Metrics:
  • Extendicare paid $570M cash for CBI Home Health, plus $13.6M in assumed IFRS 16 lease liabilities, implying total consideration of $583.6M
  • Transaction priced at 9.4x trailing EBITDA on $61.9M of adjusted EBITDA (TTM July 2025), or 8.4x including $7.4M of identified post-close synergies
  • OMERS retains majority ownership of the remaining CBI Health business (physiotherapy and rehabilitation), which accounts for the majority of CBI's remaining ~$820M in revenue
Background:

OMERS Private Equity acquired CBI Health Group from Callisto Capital LP in January 2011, marking the firm's first direct "own and control" healthcare buyout. At entry, CBI operated 134 community and hospital-based rehabilitation and healthcare facilities with approximately 3,300 clinical and support staff. The home care division was concentrated primarily in Ontario and Alberta. The deal was structured alongside co-investors Yellow Point Equity Partners (equity) and Penfund (C$70M mezzanine debt), with OMERS as lead sponsor. Yellow Point subsequently sold its full stake back to OMERS in March 2025, leaving OMERS as the sole equity holder at the time of the Extendicare sale.

Exit Rationale — Value Creation:

Over a fifteen-year holding period, OMERS transformed CBI's home care division into a national platform through four primary levers.

Workforce and volume growth.

CBI Home Health grew from 1,800 to 8,500 employees — a 4.7x increase — reaching over 10 million hours of care delivered annually at an average daily volume of approximately 28,000 hours.

Geographic expansion.

The division extended from its Ontario and Alberta base to a seven-province national footprint, significantly de-risking revenue concentration and building the scale that made it attractive to a national acquirer.

Service model innovation.

OMERS invested in moving CBI beyond traditional publicly-funded home care into higher-complexity care models, including hospital-to-home programs, integrated care delivered by interdisciplinary teams, and specialized community support services — capabilities that differentiated the business from conventional home care operators at exit.

Technology investment.

CBI Home Health built out a technology platform through a strategic partnership with AlayaCare, enabling integrated care delivery across home, clinic, and facility settings. This capability was directly cited by Extendicare as a driver of expected post-acquisition synergies.

Acquirer's Rationale:

For Extendicare, the acquisition addressed three distinct strategic gaps.

Geography.

ParaMed (Extendicare's home health care division) had limited presence in Western Canada. CBI's seven-province footprint, with particular strength in Alberta, addressed this directly.

Scale.

Combining CBI's 10 million annual hours with ParaMed's existing 24 million hours created an approximately 34-million-hour combined platform, improving purchasing leverage and enabling fixed-cost absorption across the enlarged business.

Capability.

CBI's hospital-to-home and interdisciplinary care models gave Extendicare access to higher-complexity, government-contracted service lines it did not previously operate.

Extendicare projected 9% EPS accretion at close, rising to approximately 15% with full synergy realization, and funded the acquisition at 2.7–2.9x pro forma net leverage — a conservative structure that signals management confidence in near-term free cash flow conversion.

AV
Analyst Insight
While OMERS has not disclosed financial returns, a directional assessment can be constructed from public information. The $570M cash consideration — implying total consideration of $583.6M and an exit multiple of 9.4x trailing EBITDA — was validated through a competitive, arms-length process with a public strategic buyer. The 2011 entry price was never disclosed; however, Canadian mid-market healthcare buyouts at the time typically transacted at 7–9x EBITDA, and CBI's home care division was a relatively small, geographically concentrated operation at entry. Applying reasonable assumptions on entry valuation, OMERS's ownership stake, and follow-on capital deployed (including the March 2025 buyout of Yellow Point's stake), a gross MOIC in the range of 7–9x and an implied IRR of 14–16% over the 15.25-year hold appears plausible — well above the 8–12% net IRR that Canadian pension funds typically target on private equity. See appendix for scenario assumptions. Importantly, this $570M represents only a partial realization — the retained physio/rehab platform (~$820M in remaining revenues) represents continued but unrealized value, meaning the full return on the 2011 investment remains an open picture. OMERS's own characterization, from Mark Van Wart, Head of Healthcare, was "a strong realization" and "the basis of a 15-year successful track record in healthcare investing."

Mining & Resources April 20, 2026
Agnico Eagle Mines Ltd acquires Rupert Resources Ltd for US$2.9B
All Stock, Dilutive  ·  Finland — Central Lapland Greenstone Belt
C$2.9B
All Stock · Dilutive
Deal Structure: All Stock, Dilutive
Parties and Advisors:
Target: Rupert Resources (RUP)
Advisors: BMO Capital Markets
(Fairness opinion to Rupert board)
Special Committee Valuator: Origin Merchant Partners
Buyer: Agnico Eagle (AEM)
Advisors:
  • TD Securities Inc.
  • Edgehill Advisory Ltd.
Valuation Metrics:
  • 67% premium compared to RUP's closing share price prior to the announcement, as of April 17, 2026, the last trading day prior to announcement
  • 0.0401 of AEM common share per RUP share. Each share was valued at CAD 12.083 (USD 8.824)
  • RUP shareholders receive up to C$3.00 in additional cash, paid in three C$1.00 tranches tied to hitting 5M oz in reserves, then 7.5M oz combined reserves and production, then 10M oz
Rationale:

AEM bought the rest of its stake in Rupert Resources to strengthen its operations regarding gold production. The transaction has as objective consolidating assets in Finland's Central Lapland Greenstone Belt.

This transaction complemented the acquisition of Aurion Resources and B2Gold's 70% stake at Feingold. Combining the deals, AEM now controls over a 2,492 square-kilometer land package around the Ikkari gold discovery, one of Europe's largest undeveloped deposits, improving development coordination and increasing long-term production visibility in a Tier-1 jurisdiction.

Jefferies said the move underscores AEM's strategy of building regional platforms to unlock operational synergies, rather than pursuing geographically dispersed acquisitions.

The firm's rationale is to eliminate fragmented ownership, accelerate development, and extract operational synergies of up to USD 365 million.

Overall, the deal continues the desire of AEM to control high quality jurisdictions and use Finland as its main extraction location in Europe.

AV
Analyst View
"The deal makes strategic sense as AEM already owned 13.9% of RUP. However, the Ikkari region is not yet an operating mine. Therefore, Agnico structured it so RUP shareholders only get the full C$15.00 if the project is delivered. That protects Agnico, making the deal buyer-friendly."
Jurisdiction: Ikkari Gold Discovery — Finland, Central Lapland Greenstone Belt (Tier-1)
